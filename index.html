General Rabbit Care Tips

Rabbits require a balanced diet, regular exercise, and proper grooming. Here’s what you need to know:

Diet

Rabbits should have unlimited access to fresh hay, such as timothy hay. Fresh vegetables, like leafy greens, should be added to their diet daily. Pellets are also necessary but should be limited to avoid obesity.

Exercise

Rabbits are active animals and need plenty of space to hop and explore. Set up a safe area for them to play and exercise for at least a few hours a day.

Grooming

Rabbits are naturally clean, but they need regular grooming. Long-haired breeds especially require frequent brushing to prevent matting. Check their nails monthly and trim them as necessary.

Common Rabbit Health Issues

While rabbits are generally healthy, they can develop certain issues. Here are some common health problems to watch out for:

  • Gastrointestinal Stasis: A decrease in gut motility; symptoms include loss of appetite, lethargy, and reduced fecal output. Seek immediate veterinary care.
  • Dental Issues: Rabbits' teeth grow continuously. Look for signs of drooling, difficulty eating, or weight loss.
  • Urinary Tract Problems: Watch for changes in urination habits, including blood in the urine or frequent urination.
